List of Endangered Animals

Here you can see a list of all the endangered animals you can find on our website, grouped by the continents they live on.

Europe

European Mink

This is a small, playful animal with soft brown fur and cute white spots around its nose and mouth. It loves to live near rivers and streams.

Why is it endangered?

Pollution: Dirty water from farms and factories has made it hard for the European Mink to live in clean, safe places.

Sturgeon Fish

The Sturgeon is a really old fish with a long body and tough, bumpy scales. Some Sturgeon can live for more than 100 years! People also use their eggs to make a special food called caviar.

Why is it endangered?

Overhunting: Too many Sturgeon have been caught for their eggs and meat, which has made it hard for them to survive.

Africa

Madagascan Big-headed Turtle

This is a big freshwater turtle with a very large head that can’t fit all the way into its shell. It’s one of the most endangered turtles in the whole world!

Why is it endangered?

Overhunting: These turtles are hunted a lot for food and to be sold as pets. Many are taken from the wild when they shouldn’t be.

Kihansi Spray Toad

This is a tiny, golden-yellow toad that lives in the misty water spray of Kihansi Falls in Tanzania. Instead of laying eggs, these special toads give birth to live babies!

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: A dam was built at Kihansi Falls, and it reduced the misty spray where these toads live, making it hard for them to survive.

Australia

Leadbeater’s Possum

This little possum has soft grey fur and a fluffy tail. It’s very quick and loves climbing trees at night to find food like insects and tree sap.

Why is it endangered?

Climate Change: Big bushfires are happening more often and burning down the forests where these possums live, making it harder for them to survive.

Orange-bellied Parrot

This little bird is super colorful! It has bright green feathers, a yellow chest, and a special orange spot on its belly. It’s one of the rarest parrots in the world!

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: The places where it lives, like coastal marshes and nesting areas, have been damaged by farming, building, and other human activities.

South America

Andean Bear

The Andean Bear, or Spectacled Bear, has cream markings around its eyes like glasses! It’s the only bear in South America and mostly eats plants, but sometimes eats small animals.

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: People are cutting down forests and turning the land into farms, leaving the Andean Bear with fewer places to live.

Giant Otter

The Giant Otter is a big, smooth-furred animal that loves swimming and playing. It has webbed feet and lives in families, hunting and playing together in the water.

Why is it endangered?

Pollution: Dirty water from gold mining and farming is harming the places where these otters live and find food.

North America

Black-footed Ferret

The Black-footed Ferret is a small, skinny animal with yellow fur, a black mask, and black-tipped feet and tail. It’s nocturnal and lives in prairie dog tunnels for food and shelter.

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: Farms have taken over the prairies where these ferrets live, making it harder for them to find a safe home.

Karner Blue Butterfly

The Karner Blue Butterfly is small with bright blue wings and black-and-white edges. Boys are bright blue, while girls have orange spots. They depend on wild lupine plants for food and shelter.

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: People building homes, farming, and stopping natural fires have destroyed the places where the lupine plants grow, which is where the butterflies live.

Asia

Irrawaddy Dolphin

The Irrawaddy Dolphin is a shy dolphin with a round forehead and no sharp beak. It lives in rivers and the sea and sometimes plays with people who fish.

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: Dams and other water projects are making it harder for these dolphins to live in their rivers.

Snow Leopard

The Snow Leopard is a large, quiet cat with thick, grey fur covered in black spots. It lives in cold, rocky mountains and is very good at hiding, which is why people call it the "ghost of the mountains."

Why is it endangered?

Overhunting: These leopards are hunted for their beautiful fur and other parts, which are used in traditional medicine.

Antarctica

Antarctic Fur Seal

The Antarctic Fur Seal has thick fur to stay warm in cold waters. Boys are bigger than girls and protect their space during breeding. They swim well and mostly eat krill, fish, and squid.

Why is it endangered?

Climate Change: Warmer waters and melting sea ice make it harder for them to find food, especially krill.

South Georgia Pipit

The South Georgia Pipit is a small, brown songbird that lives in the cold sub-Antarctic. Its lovely song echoes across the snowy land.

Why is it endangered?

Habitat Loss: Human activities, like research stations and tourism, can disturb the places where they breed and find food.
