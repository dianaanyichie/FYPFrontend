What is an Endangered Species?

Some animals are in big trouble because there aren’t many of them left. This is called being “endangered.” It means they could disappear forever if we don’t help. Animals become endangered when things like losing their homes, dirty air and water, too much hunting, or changes in the weather make it hard for them to survive.

How Do We Know When A Species is Endangered?

Well...

There are lots of clues to tell us if an animal is in trouble. One big clue is how many of them are left. This is called Population Size.

Population Size

If there are less than 2,500 grown-up animals left, they are called “endangered.” But if there are less than 250 grown-ups, they are in even bigger trouble and called “critically endangered.

Who Decides If An Animal is Endangered?

There’s a group called the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN). They make a special list called the “Red List.” It helps people know which animals are safe, which are in trouble, and which are almost gone forever.

How Can I Help?

There are many ways you can help endangered animals around the world.

Growing Native Plants in Garden

You can help animals by planting plants that grow naturally where you live. These plants provide food and homes for animals, like birds and butterflies, and help them stay safe.

Help in Local Cleanups

You can help animals by cleaning up trash in your neighborhood or at the park. Trash can hurt animals, so picking it up keeps their homes safe and clean

Volunteer at Shelter

You can help animals by working with people at animal shelters. Shelters take care of animals that need a home, and you can help by playing with them, feeding them, or helping keep them clean.

Visit Conservation

You can help animals by visiting places where they are being protected, like animal parks or reserves. These places keep animals safe, and by visiting, you’re helping them stay strong and healthy.

We Can Do It!

We can all work together to help save endangered animals around the world! Big companies are doing amazing work to protect animals, but you can help too! Every little action, like planting flowers, picking up trash, or learning about animals, can make a big difference.
